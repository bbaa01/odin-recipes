Steve Harvey

Life

Steve Harvey is an American television host, producer, actor, and comedian. He was born Broderick Stephen Harvey on January 17, 1957, in Welch, West Virginia. Harvey’s early life was marked by poverty and hardship, but he found solace in comedy and began performing stand-up comedy in the early 1980s. Harvey’s big break came when he became the host of “Showtime at the Apollo” in 1993. He went on to host several other TV shows, including “The Steve Harvey Show” on The WB, “Me and the Boys,” and “The Original Kings of Comedy.” He also appeared in several films, including “The Original Kings of Comedy” and “Madea Goes to Jail.” In 2010, Harvey became the host of the popular game show “Family Feud,” and he has since become known for his witty humor and charismatic hosting style. He has also hosted several other TV shows, including “The Steve Harvey Morning Show,” “Celebrity Family Feud,” and “Miss Universe.” In addition to his work in television, Harvey is also a bestselling author. He has written several books, including “Act Like a Lady, Think Like a Man” and “Jump: Take the Leap of Faith to Achieve Your Life of Abundance.” Harvey has been married three times and has seven children. He is a devout Christian and has been involved in several charitable organizations, including the Steve and Marjorie Harvey Foundation, which provides scholarships and support to underprivileged children. Throughout his career, Harvey has won numerous awards and accolades, including several Daytime Emmy Awards, a Marconi Award, and several NAACP Image Awards. He has also been inducted into the National Association of Broadcasters Hall of Fame and has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
